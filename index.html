About Mokuri
墨栗
MOKURI
A Japanese woodblock print studio
Mokuhanga is the art of Japanese woodblock printing — carving an image into wood, inking it by hand, and pressing paper to reveal the print. Each impression is unique: the grain of the wood, the weight of the ink, the texture of the paper all leave their mark.

Mokuri captures that spirit. Design your scene, carve the block, choose your inks, and pull your print — embracing the beauty of imperfection and the calm of making something by hand.
🏞️
Step One
Compose
Place elements from the library — mountains, waves, flora, fauna — onto your paper. Set the atmosphere with sky and ground. Arrange, resize, and layer to design your scene.
🪵
Step Two
Carve
Use fine, V, and U gouges to carve detail into the woodblock. Set the carve level for each element, then draw freehand strokes to add texture and character. Focus on an element to carve up close.
🎨
Step Three
Ink
Choose from seven traditional palettes — Sumi, Edo, Hokusai, and more. Customize each element's color zones and add bokashi gradients for soft ink transitions.
📜
Step Four
Make Print
Select your paper — smooth Hosho, fibrous Kozo, or warm Torinoko. Adjust ink weight and impressions, then pull your print. Every pull is unique.
